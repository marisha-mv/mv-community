The Problem

Traditional community platforms for online courses focus on discussion and connection. But here's the uncomfortable truth:

"People don't want to connect about quests. They want to complete them."

Forcing social interaction creates friction, not motivation. People feel:

  • "My meditation journey is personal. I don't want to discuss it with strangers."
  • "I barely have time for the lessons. Now I have to socialize too?"
  • "Everyone else seems to 'get it' and I'm struggling."

The Solution: Activity-First Community

Think Strava, not WhatsApp. A public activity feed where completion is celebrated, accountability is passive, and connection is opt-in.
